Research works

My research work mainly has been focused on multilingual learning and learning in low-resource settings. Couple of research projects that I have completed are:

MMT: A Multilingual and Multi-Topic Indian Social Media Dataset

Dwip Dalal, Vivek Srivastava, Mayank Singh. Reserach Paper (submitted to CIKM conference for publication)

Made a large-scale multilingual and multi-topic dataset (MMT) collected from Twitter (≈1.7 million Tweets), encompassing 13 coarse-grained and 63 fine-grained topics in the Indian context. Further, we made a subset (≈5.5k Tweets) by annotating tweets from the MMT dataset with various Indian languages and their code-mixed counterparts. The annotation was performed so as to remove errors that might have occurred because of the mistakes in language detection by Twitter API.
Multiple topic modeling and language identification experiments were performed on these datasets to check the ability of these models in capturing linguistic diversity. We have used both statistical and context-based topic models. Based on these experiments, we concluded that models don’t perform quite well in multilingual and multi-topic scenarios. Therefore, we are making this dataset publicly available because we believe that the availability of such a large-scale and quality dataset will be useful in building systems for numerous downstream tasks such as multilingual topic-modeling, language identification, machine translation, etc.

Code-Mixed Question-Answer Pair Generation

Dwip Dalal, Vivek Srivastava, Mayank Singh. Paper under review for publication

Working on creating a large-scale question-answer dataset in the Hindi language. Since it would not be humanly feasible to annotate such large-scale datasets, so in this case, we performed fine tunning of pre-existing language models using a mix of Hindi QA pair datasets like Xsquad, MLQA, CHaii, etc. Multiple different language models like mbret, mt5, xlm-Roberta, etc., were fine tuned, and then the quality of generated QA pair was compared. Based on these results, we choose the final model.
To increase the dataset for fine-tuning, we also performed the translation of the available Squad dataset. It was followed by paraphrasing in an attempt to improve the quality of the generated sentences.
